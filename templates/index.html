What this is

Ademi is a learning sabbatical — a structured pause to go deep on things I care about, one book at a time. The rule is simple: I don't move to the next chapter until I can explain the current one clearly enough that a curious stranger could follow it.

The method comes from Richard Feynman. If you can't explain something simply, you haven't understood it yet — you've only memorised the words. So that's the test. Write it out from scratch. No formulas without intuition behind them. No jargon without a plain-language translation.

I've built courses before and loved that work — the challenge of making something genuinely hard feel accessible, finding the right analogy, knowing when a diagram says more than a paragraph. That same energy lives here, but slower and more honest. Just me, the source material, and the writing.

If you want to master something, teach it.

— Richard P. Feynman
